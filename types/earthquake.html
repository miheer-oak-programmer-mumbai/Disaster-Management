Earthquakes

Overview

Earthquakes are sudden ground movements caused by the release of stress along geological faults. They can produce strong shaking, surface rupture, and trigger secondary hazards such as landslides and tsunamis.

Causes

  • Tectonic plate movement and fault rupture.

Impacts

  • Building collapse, infrastructure damage, casualties, and service disruption.

Preparedness

  • Retrofit buildings, secure heavy furniture, practice drop-cover-hold drills.

Example

2015 Nepal Earthquake: A major event demonstrating the importance of resilient construction and rapid humanitarian response.
